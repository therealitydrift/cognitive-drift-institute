# Cognitive Drift Series

*A. Jacobs — Reality Drift Framework (2023–2026)*

## Overview

The Cognitive Drift Series presents a structured model of cognition, meaning, and failure under conditions of increasing complexity, compression, and scale.

These papers describe how systems—human or artificial—can remain coherent and functional while gradually losing alignment with underlying reality.

This section of the series focuses on recursive cognition, co-cognition with artificial intelligence, and the evolution of consciousness under externalized feedback systems.

---

## Core Idea

Cognitive Drift emerges when:

- Compression increases  
- Recursion accelerates  
- Semantic fidelity degrades  
- Constraint and judgment weaken  

Under these conditions, systems continue to produce coherent outputs while losing grounding in reality.

---

## Structure of the Series

### CD 4.3 — Synthetic Flow: The Co-Cognition Loop

Introduces synthetic flow as a stabilized recursive feedback loop between human cognition and machine compression, where meaning is refined through iterative co-processing.

### CD 5.1 — Recursive Compression Theory

Defines intelligence as compression with memory and consciousness as recursive self-modeling, establishing the core mechanism behind system stability and drift.

### CD 5.2 — The Consciousness Timeline

Traces the evolution of consciousness through layers of recursive compression, from embodied systems to language, media, networks, and AI.

### CD 5.3 — Consciousness Drift Cycles

Explores how meaning systems degrade over time as fidelity declines, and how new recursive structures emerge to restore coherence.

### CD 5.4 — Consciousness and Recursive Transitions

Examines how external systems—ritual, language, media, and AI—reshape how recursive loops stabilize awareness across historical phases.

### CD 6.1 — Language and Symbolic Constraint

Explains how language enables persistent recursive thought while introducing the conditions for self-maintaining symbolic systems and drift.

### CD 6.2 — Linear vs Recursive Thinking

Distinguishes between sequential and loop-based cognition, showing how recursive thinkers engage AI as a feedback system rather than a tool.

## Key Concepts

- **Recursive Compression** — Iterative reduction and reuse of representations  
- **Co-Cognition** — Shared cognitive processing between human and machine  
- **Synthetic Flow** — Stabilized recursive feedback between human and AI  
- **Semantic Fidelity** — Alignment between representation and reality  
- **Constraint** — Mechanism that stabilizes recursive loops  
- **Cognitive Drift** — Misalignment emerging from unbounded recursion  

---

## What This Explains

This segment of the Cognitive Drift Series accounts for:

- Why AI can feel like a thinking partner rather than a tool  
- Why recursive thinkers experience modern systems differently  
- How consciousness evolves through externalized feedback systems  
- Why meaning destabilizes under accelerating symbolic environments  
- How coherent systems drift without breaking  

---

## Relationship to Reality Drift

Cognitive Drift describes the internal mechanism behind Reality Drift.

Where Reality Drift appears at the level of systems, institutions, and culture, Cognitive Drift explains how the same structural pattern emerges within cognition itself—especially as recursive processes become externalized through artificial intelligence.

## Notes

This portion of the series focuses on the transition from internal cognition to distributed and co-cognitive systems.

Artificial intelligence introduces a new layer of recursive processing, extending cognition beyond the individual and increasing both the potential for stabilization and the risk of drift.

Cognitive Drift is not treated as an anomaly, but as an expected outcome when recursive systems scale without sufficient constraint to preserve semantic fidelity.
