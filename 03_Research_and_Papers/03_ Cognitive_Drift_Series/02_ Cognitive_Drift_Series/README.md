# Cognitive Drift Series

*A. Jacobs — Reality Drift Framework (2023–2026)*

## Overview

The Cognitive Drift Series presents a structured model of cognition, meaning, and failure under conditions of increasing complexity, compression, and scale.

These papers describe how systems—human or artificial—can remain coherent and functional while gradually losing alignment with underlying reality.

This series forms the cognitive and mechanistic layer of the broader Reality Drift Framework.

---

## Core Idea

Cognitive Drift emerges when:

- Compression increases  
- Recursion accelerates  
- Semantic fidelity degrades  
- Constraint and judgment weaken  

Under these conditions, systems continue to produce coherent outputs while losing grounding in reality.

---

#### Structure of the Series

### CD 3.1 — Cognitive Compression Styles

Examines how minds compress reality under constraint, introducing cognitive compression architectures and their role in shaping meaning, stability, and drift.  

### CD 3.2 — Cognitive Porousness and Boundary Permeability

Explores how permeability between internal cognition and external systems enables co-cognition while increasing susceptibility to cognitive drift.  

### CD 3.3 — The 5%: High-Compression Cognition and Co-Cognition

Describes a subset of high-compression minds whose recursive cognitive structures become more legible through interaction with AI.  

### CD 4.1 — How AI Mirrors the Mind

Demonstrates how artificial intelligence reveals the structure of human cognition through compression, prediction, recursion, and semantic fidelity.  

### CD 4.2 — Distributed Cognition and Shared Predictive Systems

Explains how predictive processing becomes distributed across human and machine systems, producing shared models of reality and scaling cognitive drift.

## Key Concepts

- **Compression** — Reduction of complexity into usable representations  
- **Recursion** — Feedback of representations into the system  
- **Semantic Fidelity** — Alignment between representation and reality  
- **Judgment** — Constraint that forces closure and decision  
- **Drift Principle** — Misalignment that emerges when compression exceeds capacity  
- **Cognitive Drift** — Emergent failure mode when these relationships destabilize  

---

## What This Explains

The Cognitive Drift model accounts for:

- Why modern life feels coherent but unstable  
- Why AI produces fluent but ungrounded outputs  
- Why decisions feel delayed or non-final  
- Why meaning becomes harder to maintain under information overload  

---

## Relationship to Reality Drift

Cognitive Drift describes the internal mechanism behind Reality Drift.

Where Reality Drift appears at the level of systems, institutions, and culture, Cognitive Drift explains how the same structural pattern emerges within cognition itself.

## Notes

This series is part of an ongoing effort to formalize the structural dynamics of cognition under modern information conditions.

Cognitive Drift is not treated as an anomaly or pathology, but as an expected outcome when compression, scale, and optimization exceed a system’s ability to maintain semantic fidelity and constraint.
